COMPANY LEAVE AND ATTENDANCE POLICY

Last Updated: January 2025
Version: 3.2

================================================================================
SECTION 1: ANNUAL LEAVE POLICY
================================================================================

1.1 ENTITLEMENT
- All full-time employees are entitled to 20 days of paid annual leave per year
- Part-time employees receive prorated leave based on hours worked
- Leave accrues at a rate of 1.67 days per month of service
- New employees can begin using accrued leave after completing 90 days of employment

1.2 REQUEST PROCESS
- All annual leave requests must be submitted through the HR portal
- Requests should be made at least 2 weeks in advance for manager approval
- Manager will approve or deny within 3 business days
- Emergency leave requests will be considered on a case-by-case basis

1.3 CARRYOVER POLICY
- Maximum carryover: 5 days to the next calendar year
- Carryover days must be used by March 31st of the following year
- Unused leave beyond the carryover limit will be forfeited
- No cash compensation for forfeited leave

1.4 BLACKOUT PERIODS
- Certain peak business periods may have restricted leave availability
- Department managers will communicate blackout periods at least 60 days in advance
- Emergency situations will be handled with discretion

================================================================================
SECTION 2: SICK LEAVE POLICY
================================================================================

2.1 ENTITLEMENT
- Employees receive 10 days of paid sick leave annually
- Sick leave is available from the first day of employment
- Unused sick leave does not carry over to the next year
- Sick leave cannot be encashed upon resignation or termination

2.2 USAGE GUIDELINES
- Sick leave is for personal illness, injury, or medical appointments
- Can be used for care of immediate family members (spouse, children, parents)
- Medical certificate required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days
- Doctor's note may be requested for patterns of suspicious absences

2.3 NOTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS
- Notify your manager as soon as possible, preferably before 10:00 AM
- For planned medical appointments, provide at least 48 hours notice
- Update absence in HR portal on the same day or next business day
- For extended illness, provide weekly updates to manager and HR

SECTION 3: PUBLIC HOLIDAYS
================================================================================

3.1 OBSERVED HOLIDAYS
The company observes the following national and company-specific holidays:
- New Year's Day
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day
- Presidents' Day
- Memorial Day
- Independence Day
- Labor Day
- Thanksgiving Day and Friday after Thanksgiving
- Christmas Eve and Christmas Day
- New Year's Eve

3.2 HOLIDAY WORK POLICY
- If required to work on a public holiday, compensatory leave will be granted
- Compensation is 1.5 days off for each holiday worked
- Must be approved in advance by department manager
- Compensatory leave must be used within 3 months

================================================================================
SECTION 4: PARENTAL LEAVE
================================================================================

4.1 MATERNITY LEAVE
- 16 weeks of paid leave for birth mothers
- Leave can begin up to 2 weeks before due date
- Must provide medical documentation of pregnancy
- Benefits continue during leave period

4.2 PATERNITY LEAVE
- 2 weeks of paid leave for fathers
- Must be taken within 6 months of child's birth
- Can be taken consecutively or split into two separate weeks
- Requires birth certificate or adoption papers

4.3 ADOPTION LEAVE
- 12 weeks of paid leave for primary caregiver in adoption
- 2 weeks for secondary caregiver
- Applies to domestic and international adoptions
- Requires adoption agency documentation

4.4 PARENTAL LEAVE BENEFITS
- All benefits (health insurance, 401k matching) continue during leave
- Accrual of vacation time continues during paid parental leave
- Position is guaranteed upon return from leave
- Flexible return-to-work schedule can be discussed with manager

================================================================================
SECTION 5: ATTENDANCE REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

5.1 STANDARD WORK HOURS
- Standard work week: Monday to Friday
- Standard hours: 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM (with 1-hour lunch break)
- Total: 40 hours per week
- Core hours (must be present or online): 10:00 AM - 4:00 PM

5.2 FLEXIBLE ARRIVAL
- Flexible arrival window: 8:00 AM - 10:00 AM
- Departure time adjusts accordingly to complete 8 hours
- Core hours must be maintained regardless of arrival time
- Consistent late arrival (after 10:00 AM) may affect performance reviews

5.3 TARDINESS POLICY
- Arrival after 10:00 AM is considered tardy
- Manager notification required for tardiness
- Excessive tardiness (more than 5 instances per month) may result in:
  * Verbal warning (first occurrence)
  * Written warning (second occurrence)
  * Performance improvement plan (third occurrence)
  * Potential termination (continued pattern)

5.4 UNAUTHORIZED ABSENCE
- Absence without notification is considered unauthorized
- More than 3 unauthorized absences in a 6-month period may result in:
  * Written warning
  * Suspension without pay
  * Termination of employment
- Job abandonment defined as 3 consecutive days of no-call, no-show

================================================================================
SECTION 6: REMOTE WORK POLICY
================================================================================

6.1 ELIGIBILITY
- Available to employees after 6 months of employment
- Must have satisfactory performance review
- Role must be suitable for remote work
- Manager approval required

6.2 REMOTE WORK GUIDELINES
- Maximum 2 days per week remote work (hybrid model)
- Must maintain same productivity and availability standards
- Core hours must be observed
- Required to attend in-person meetings when scheduled
- Equipment and secure internet connection required

6.3 APPLICATION PROCESS
- Submit remote work request through HR portal
- Manager reviews and approves/denies within 5 business days
- Remote work arrangement reviewed quarterly
- Can be revoked if performance or availability concerns arise

================================================================================
SECTION 7: OTHER LEAVE TYPES
================================================================================

7.1 BEREAVEMENT LEAVE
- 5 days paid leave for death of immediate family member
- 3 days paid leave for death of extended family member
- Immediate family: spouse, children, parents, siblings
- Extended family: grandparents, grandchildren, in-laws
- Additional unpaid leave available upon request

7.2 JURY DUTY
- Paid leave for duration of jury service
- Must provide jury summons to HR
- Any compensation from court should be reported
- Expected to return to work if dismissed early

7.3 MILITARY LEAVE
- Unpaid leave for military service obligations
- Job protection as required by USERRA
- Benefits continuation as required by law
- Must provide military orders to HR

7.4 PERSONAL LEAVE (UNPAID)
- Available for personal circumstances not covered by other leave types
- Requires manager and HR approval
- Maximum 30 days per year
- Must exhaust all paid leave first
- No guarantee of benefits continuation during extended unpaid leave

================================================================================
SECTION 8: LEAVE ABUSE AND DISCIPLINARY ACTION
================================================================================

8.1 LEAVE ABUSE DEFINITION
- Patterns of suspicious absences (e.g., always Monday/Friday)
- Using sick leave when not actually sick
- Providing false information or documentation
- Failing to follow proper notification procedures

8.2 INVESTIGATION PROCESS
- HR will investigate suspected leave abuse
- Employee will be given opportunity to explain
- Medical certification may be required for future sick leave
- Disciplinary action may include written warning, suspension, or termination

8.3 DOCUMENTATION
- All leave requests and approvals are maintained by HR
- Medical certificates kept confidentially in employee file
- Leave balances available in employee self-service portal
