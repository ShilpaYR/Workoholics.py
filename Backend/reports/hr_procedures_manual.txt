SECTION 1: EMPLOYEE ONBOARDING PROCEDURES
================================================================================

1.1 PRE-BOARDING PHASE (1 Week Before Start Date)

Responsibilities: HR Team
- Send welcome email to new hire with:
  * First day schedule and arrival time
  * Dress code information
  * Required documents to bring (ID, banking info, certifications)
  * Parking/transportation information
  * Emergency contact information
  
- Prepare workspace:
  * Request desk assignment from facilities
  * Order computer equipment from IT
  * Request phone extension
  * Order business cards
  * Set up email account
  
- Assign onboarding buddy:
  * Select buddy from same department
  * Brief buddy on responsibilities
  * Share new hire's background with buddy
  
- Prepare onboarding checklist and materials
- Schedule first-week meetings and training sessions

Responsibilities: Manager
- Review new hire's resume and background
- Prepare 30-60-90 day goals
- Inform team of new hire's arrival
- Plan first-week projects and assignments

1.2 FIRST DAY PROCEDURES

8:30 AM - HR Orientation (Conference Room A)
- Welcome and introduction to company
- Complete I-9 Employment Eligibility Verification
- Complete W-4 tax withholding forms
- Complete state tax forms
- Benefits enrollment overview and selection
- Sign employee handbook acknowledgment
- Review company policies and code of conduct
- Provide employee ID badge
- Tour of facilities

10:30 AM - IT Setup (IT Department)
- Receive computer and equipment
- Set up email and system accounts
- Configure phone and voicemail
- Review security policies and sign acceptable use agreement
- Training on company systems and software

12:00 PM - Lunch with Onboarding Buddy
- Informal introduction to company culture
- Tour of local lunch options
- Answer questions in relaxed setting

1:00 PM - Department Introduction (Manager's Office)
- Introduction to team members
- Overview of department goals and structure
- Tour of department workspace
- Assignment of initial projects
- Schedule regular check-in meetings

3:00 PM - First Assignments (At Desk)
- Begin initial projects
- Complete online training modules
- Review department documentation

4:30 PM - End of First Day Check-in (Manager)
- Review day's activities
- Answer questions
- Confirm schedule for rest of week

1.3 FIRST WEEK PROCEDURES

Day 2:
- Complete required compliance training (harassment prevention, safety)
- Meet with key cross-functional team members
- Shadow onboarding buddy

Day 3:
- Deep dive into role responsibilities with manager
- Access to necessary systems and tools verified
- Begin working on starter projects

Day 4:
- Meeting with department head
- Review of 30-60-90 day goals
- Q&A session with HR about benefits

Day 5:
- First week reflection meeting with manager
- Confirm understanding of expectations
- Address any concerns or questions
- Week-end feedback session

1.4 FIRST 90 DAYS MILESTONES

Week 2-4:
- Weekly 1-on-1 meetings with manager
- Complete all required training modules
- Meet with skip-level manager (manager's manager)
- Attend department meetings

30-Day Check-in:
- Formal meeting with HR and manager
- Review progress on initial goals
- Address any adjustment issues
- Verify benefits enrollment is complete

60-Day Check-in:
- Progress review with manager
- Adjust goals if necessary
- Identify development opportunities
- Expand project responsibilities

90-Day Review:
- Formal performance review
- Evaluation of probationary period
- Decision on continued employment
- Set goals for next quarter
- Discussion of career development path

SECTION 2: PERFORMANCE REVIEW PROCEDURES
================================================================================

2.1 REVIEW SCHEDULE

Annual Performance Review Cycle:
- Mid-Year Review: June 1 - July 15
- Annual Review: December 1 - January 15

Timeline:
Week 1: HR announces review period and deadlines
Week 2-3: Employees complete self-assessments
Week 3-4: Managers complete evaluations
Week 5: Calibration meetings
Week 6: Review meetings with employees
Week 7: HR processes documentation and compensation changes

2.2 SELF-ASSESSMENT PROCESS (Employee)

Instructions for Employees:
1. Log into HR portal and access self-assessment form
2. Review your goals and objectives from last review period
3. Complete all sections:
   - Accomplishments and achievements
   - Progress on goals
   - Challenges faced and how overcome
   - Areas for improvement
   - Career development interests
   - Goals for next period
   
4. Provide specific examples and metrics where possible
5. Submit self-assessment at least 48 hours before review meeting
6. Prepare questions and discussion points for meeting

Deadline: Submit by end of Week 3 of review cycle

2.3 MANAGER EVALUATION PROCESS

Step 1: Review employee's self-assessment
Step 2: Gather input from:
- Direct observations
- Project outcomes and deliverables
- Peer feedback (if 360 review)
- Attendance and punctuality records
- Any documented incidents or achievements

Step 3: Complete evaluation form in HR system:
- Rate performance on each competency (1-5 scale)
- Overall performance rating (1-5 scale)
- Written comments for each section
- Goals and development plan for next period
- Promotion or compensation change recommendations

Step 4: Submit evaluation for calibration

2.4 PERFORMANCE RATING SCALE

5 - EXCEPTIONAL
- Consistently exceeds expectations in all areas
- Demonstrates leadership beyond role requirements
- Makes significant impact on company success
- Serves as role model for others
- Typical distribution: ~5% of employees

4 - EXCEEDS EXPECTATIONS
- Frequently surpasses goals and objectives
- Takes initiative beyond assigned duties
- Delivers high-quality work consistently
- Demonstrates growth and development
- Typical distribution: ~15% of employees

3 - MEETS EXPECTATIONS
- Reliably achieves all goals and objectives
- Performs all job responsibilities competently
- Meets quality and deadline requirements
- Contributes positively to team
- Typical distribution: ~70% of employees

2 - NEEDS IMPROVEMENT
- Sometimes meets expectations but inconsistent
- Requires additional supervision or support
- Has gaps in skills or performance
- May require performance improvement plan
- Typical distribution: ~8% of employees

1 - UNSATISFACTORY
- Rarely meets expectations
- Significant performance deficiencies
- Improvement plan required
- May lead to termination if no improvement
- Typical distribution: ~2% of employees

2.5 CALIBRATION MEETING

Purpose: Ensure fairness and consistency in ratings across the organization

Participants:
- Department managers
- HR Business Partner
- Department VP or Director

Process:
1. Review distribution of ratings across department
2. Discuss employees with ratings of 4, 5, 1, and 2
3. Challenge ratings that seem inconsistent
4. Review promotion and compensation recommendations
5. Ensure ratings align with demonstrated performance
6. Adjust ratings if necessary for fairness

Duration: 2-3 hours per department

2.6 PERFORMANCE REVIEW MEETING

Preparation (Manager):
- Schedule 60-minute meeting in private location
- Review finalized evaluation
- Prepare specific examples and feedback
- Review employee's self-assessment
- Have development resources ready

Meeting Agenda:
1. Opening (5 min)
   - Set positive tone
   - Outline meeting structure
   
2. Employee Self-Assessment Discussion (10 min)
   - Employee shares key points
   - Manager asks clarifying questions
   
3. Performance Review (25 min)
   - Review each competency area
   - Share specific examples
   - Discuss rating and rationale
   - Share overall performance rating
   
4. Development Discussion (10 min)
   - Strengths to leverage
   - Areas for growth
   - Development opportunities
   - Training or resources needed
   
5. Goal Setting (10 min)
   - Review and agree on goals for next period
   - Ensure goals are SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
   - Document action steps
   
6. Closing (5 min)
   - Compensation changes (if applicable)
   - Next steps
   - Questions and concerns
   - Employee acknowledgment

Post-Meeting:
- Employee signs review acknowledgment in HR system
- Manager documents meeting notes
- HR processes compensation changes

2.7 PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLAN (PIP)

When Required:
- Performance rating of 2 (Needs Improvement) or 1 (Unsatisfactory)
- Consistent failure to meet job expectations
- Behavioral issues affecting performance

PIP Process:
1. Meeting with employee, manager, and HR
2. Document specific performance deficiencies
3. Set clear, measurable improvement goals
4. Define timeline (typically 30, 60, or 90 days)
5. Identify support and resources to be provided
6. Schedule regular check-in meetings (weekly)
7. Document progress at each check-in

Outcomes:
- Successful completion: Return to normal performance management
- Partial improvement: Extend PIP with modified goals
- No improvement: Termination recommended

Documentation: All PIP documents maintained in employee file

SECTION 3: LEAVE REQUEST PROCEDURES
================================================================================

3.1 ANNUAL LEAVE REQUEST PROCESS

Step 1: Employee Planning
- Check leave balance in HR portal
- Review team calendar for conflicts
- Consider business needs and project deadlines
- Plan at least 2 weeks in advance when possible

Step 2: Submit Request
- Log into HR portal: https://hr.company.com
- Navigate to "Leave Request"
- Select leave type: Annual Leave
- Enter start and end dates
- Add notes if necessary
- Submit request

Step 3: Manager Review
- Manager receives email notification
- Reviews team calendar and business needs
- Approves or denies within 3 business days
- If denied, provides reason and suggests alternative dates

Step 4: Confirmation
- Employee receives email notification of decision
- Approved leave automatically added to team calendar
- Employee receives calendar invitation
- HR system automatically deducts from leave balance

Emergency Leave Requests:
- Call or text manager as soon as possible
- Submit request in HR portal when able
- Manager will approve retroactively
- May require explanation for last-minute request

3.2 SICK LEAVE NOTIFICATION PROCESS

Same-Day Sick Leave:
1. Notify manager before 10:00 AM (call, text, or email)
2. Message should include:
   - That you're taking sick leave
   - Expected duration (one day or longer)
   - Whether you'll be reachable
   - Any urgent matters that need coverage
   
3. Log sick leave in HR portal same day or next business day
4. Manager marks employee as "Out Sick" in team calendar

Extended Sick Leave (more than 3 days):
1. Follow same-day notification process
2. Provide daily or every-other-day updates to manager
3. Medical certificate required for return to work
4. Submit medical certificate to HR (confidential)
5. Discuss any needed accommodations upon return

Medical Appointments:
- Request time off through HR portal at least 48 hours in advance
- Can use sick leave or flexible hours
- Only brief appointments require notification, not formal approval
- Extended appointments (half-day or more) should be formally requested

3.3 PARENTAL LEAVE REQUEST PROCESS

Maternity Leave:
1. Notify manager and HR as soon as possible (ideally 3 months before due date)
2. Complete Parental Leave Request Form in HR portal
3. Provide expected due date and desired leave start date
4. HR will schedule meeting to review:
   - Leave duration (16 weeks)
   - Benefits continuation
   - Return to work planning
   - FMLA paperwork if applicable
   
5. Submit medical documentation of pregnancy
6. HR processes request and confirms leave dates
7. Two weeks before leave, meet with manager for transition planning
8. Upon baby's arrival, notify HR with birth date
9. Four weeks before planned return, HR contacts to confirm return date

Paternity/Adoption Leave:
1. Notify manager and HR at least 4 weeks before expected leave
2. Complete Parental Leave Request Form
3. Provide expected arrival/placement date
4. Review leave entitlement (2 weeks paternity, 12 weeks adoption)
5. Submit birth certificate or adoption papers to HR after event
6. Coordinate return date with manager and HR

3.4 OTHER LEAVE TYPES

Bereavement Leave:
- Notify manager immediately
- Contact HR within 24 hours
- Provide relationship to deceased
- Leave granted immediately, paperwork can follow
- Death certificate may be requested for records

Jury Duty:
- Notify manager and HR as soon as summons received
- Provide copy of jury summons to HR
- Update manager daily on jury duty status
- Submit any court payment documentation to HR
- If dismissed early, expected to return to work

Military Leave:
- Notify manager and HR as soon as orders received
- Provide copy of military orders to HR
- HR will coordinate USERRA compliance
- Benefits continuation arranged by HR
- Reemployment rights protected by law

Personal Leave (Unpaid):
- Submit written request to manager and HR at least 30 days in advance
- Include:
  * Reason for leave
  * Requested duration
  * Work transition plan
- Manager and HR review and approve/deny
- Approval requires exhausting all paid leave first
- Discuss benefits continuation with HR

SECTION 4: SALARY AND COMPENSATION PROCEDURES
================================================================================

4.1 ANNUAL MERIT INCREASE PROCESS

Timeline: January - February

Week 1-2 (January):
- HR completes market analysis
- Leadership determines overall merit budget
- Budget allocated to departments based on headcount and performance distribution

Week 3-4 (January):
- Managers receive merit guidelines and budget allocation
- Managers prepare merit recommendations based on:
  * Performance review ratings
  * Current salary vs. market range
  * Internal equity
  * Time since last increase
  
Week 5 (January):
- Managers submit recommendations to HR
- HR reviews for compliance with guidelines and budget
- Meetings held to discuss any discrepancies

Week 6 (February):
- Final approvals from department VPs
- HR processes merit increases in payroll system
- Effective date: First pay period in February

Week 7-8 (February):
- Managers communicate increases to employees
- Written confirmation sent by HR
- Updated compensation information in employee portal

4.2 PROMOTION PROCESS

Step 1: Manager Identifies Candidate
- Evaluates readiness for next level
- Reviews competencies and performance history
- Discusses with HR Business Partner

Step 2: Promotion Proposal
Manager prepares written proposal including:
- Employee name and current role/level
- Proposed new role/level
- Business justification
- Performance evidence
- Skills demonstration
- Impact on team and organization
- Proposed compensation increase

Step 3: HR Review
- Verify meets criteria for new level
- Assess internal equity impact
- Confirm budget availability
- Market data analysis for new role

Step 4: Approval Process
- Department VP approval
- HR Director approval
- CFO approval (for grade 5 and above)

Step 5: Communication
- Manager meets with employee to announce promotion
- Discuss new responsibilities and expectations
- Review compensation changes
- HR sends written confirmation
- Update in HRIS effective next month 1st

Typical Promotion Increase: 8-15% of base salary

4.3 OFF-CYCLE ADJUSTMENTS

Reasons for Off-Cycle Adjustments:
- Market correction (salary significantly below market)
- Retention risk (competing offer)
- Material change in role responsibilities
- Internal equity correction
- Acquisition of new critical skills

Process:
1. Manager discusses situation with HR Business Partner
2. Gather supporting data:
   - Market analysis
   - Performance evidence
   - Competing offer details (if applicable)
   - Internal comparisons
   
3. Manager prepares business case
4. Submit for approval:
   - HR Director
   - CFO
   - CEO (for senior roles or large increases)
   
5. If approved:
   - Process effective 1st of following month
   - Manager communicates to employee
   - HR sends written confirmation

Timing: Typically takes 2-4 weeks from submission to approval

4.4 SALARY HISTORY AND RECORDS

Employee Access:
- View current salary in employee self-service portal
- View salary history for last 5 years
- Access annual compensation statements

Manager Access:
- View current salaries of direct reports
- Access to team's salary history
- Market data for roles in department

Confidentiality:
- All salary information is confidential
- Disclosure of another employee's salary is grounds for discipline
- Questions about pay should be directed to HR or manager

SECTION 5: TERMINATION PROCEDURES
================================================================================

5.1 VOLUNTARY RESIGNATION

Employee Responsibilities:
1. Provide written resignation letter to manager
2. Minimum notice period: 2 weeks (4 weeks recommended for senior roles)
3. Include last working date in resignation letter
4. Maintain professionalism during notice period

Manager Responsibilities:
1. Accept resignation letter and forward to HR immediately
2. Schedule exit interview with HR
3. Create knowledge transfer plan
4. Inform team appropriately
5. Ensure return of company property

HR Responsibilities:
1. Schedule exit interview
2. Process resignation in HRIS
3. Calculate final pay including unused vacation
4. Prepare COBRA information
5. Provide information on final paycheck, benefits end date, 401(k) options
6. Arrange return of company property
7. Deactivate system access on last day

Knowledge Transfer:
- Document critical processes and procedures
- Train replacement or team members
- Complete handoff of active projects
- Provide contact information for outstanding items

Last Day:
- Return laptop, phone, ID badge, keys, credit card
- Remove personal items
- Exit interview with HR
- System access deactivated end of business day
- Final paycheck issued according to state law

5.2 INVOLUNTARY TERMINATION

Pre-Termination Requirements:
- Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) typically precedes termination for performance
- Documentation of performance issues
- Evidence of coaching and support provided
- Approval chain:
  * Manager recommendation
  * HR Business Partner review
  * Department VP approval
  * HR Director approval
  * Legal review (for potential risk cases)

Termination Meeting:
Participants:
- Employee's manager
- HR representative
- Optional: Additional witness

Meeting Process:
1. Schedule meeting at end of day (ideally Friday)
2. Manager delivers termination message (brief, clear, respectful)
3. State reason for termination
4. HR explains final pay, benefits, and next steps
5. Keep meeting short (15-20 minutes)
6. Escort employee to collect personal belongings
7. Collect company property
8. Escort from building

Immediate Actions:
- Deactivate all system access immediately
- Disable ID badge
- Notify IT, Security, Facilities
- Change passwords for shared accounts
- Notify team appropriately

Post-Termination:
- Process final paycheck according to state law
- Send COBRA information within 14 days
- Provide information on 401(k) rollover options
- Mail any remaining personal items if not collected
- Document termination in employee file

5.3 LAYOFF/REDUCTION IN FORCE

Process:
1. Business decision requiring headcount reduction
2. Executive team determines scope and criteria
3. HR works with leadership to select impacted positions
4. Legal review of selection criteria
5. Prepare severance packages
6. Schedule notification meetings

Notification Meeting:
- Manager and HR meet with affected employee
- Deliver message clearly and compassionately
- Explain that position is eliminated (not performance-based)
- Review severance package
- Explain benefits continuation
- Provide outplacement services information
- Answer questions

Severance Package Typically Includes:
- 2 weeks pay per year of service (minimum 4 weeks)
- Health benefits continuation for severance period
- Outplacement services (resume, job search, coaching)
- Accrued vacation payout
- Pro-rated bonus (if close to payout date)

Post-Layoff Support:
- Access to outplacement services for 6 months
- Alumni network invitation
- Positive reference provided
- Consideration for rehire if positions become available

SECTION 6: INTERNAL TRANSFER PROCEDURES
================================================================================

6.1 ELIGIBILITY

Employees are eligible for internal transfer if:
- At least 6 months in current position
- Meeting or exceeding performance expectations
- Not currently on performance improvement plan
- No active disciplinary issues

6.2 INTERNAL TRANSFER PROCESS

Step 1: Self-Assessment and Preparation
- Review internal job postings on career portal
- Assess qualifications and interest
- Update internal resume/profile
- Prepare for potential career change

Step 2: Manager Discussion
- Employee should discuss transfer interest with current manager
- Provide context for interest in new role
- This step is recommended but not required
- Manager should be supportive of employee development

Step 3: Application
- Apply through internal career portal
- Complete application and attach updated resume
- System automatically notifies current manager
- Application routed to hiring manager

Step 4: Interview Process
- Hiring manager reviews application
- If interested, schedules interview
- Standard interview process applies
- May include multiple rounds

Step 5: Selection Decision
- Hiring manager makes selection decision
- If selected, hiring manager contacts current manager
- Current and new managers discuss transition timing
- Typically 2-4 weeks transition period

Step 6: Transition
- Employee and managers create transition plan
- Knowledge transfer to backfill or team
- Move date confirmed
- HR updates records and systems
- Welcome process in new department

6.3 LATERAL VS. PROMOTIONAL TRANSFERS

Lateral Move:
- Same job level and pay grade
- Typically no compensation change
- Good for skill development and career exploration

Promotional Move:
- Higher job level and pay grade
- Salary increase typically 8-15%
- Must demonstrate readiness for next level

6.4 MANAGER RESPONSIBILITIES

Current Manager:
- Support employee development
- Don't block reasonable transfer opportunities
- Provide honest feedback if asked by hiring manager
- Work collaboratively on transition timing

Hiring Manager:
- Treat internal and external candidates fairly
- Contact current manager after selection
- Work collaboratively on transition plan
- Provide onboarding for new team member

SECTION 7: GRIEVANCE AND COMPLAINT PROCEDURES
================================================================================

7.1 INFORMAL RESOLUTION (Step 1)

When to Use:
- First step for most workplace concerns
- Issues with manager, coworker, or working conditions
- Before formal complaint

Process:
1. Employee discusses concern directly with involved party if comfortable
2. If not resolved or not comfortable with direct discussion, speak with immediate manager
3. Clearly explain the issue and desired resolution
4. Manager has 5 business days to:
   - Investigate situation
   - Speak with relevant parties
   - Propose resolution
   - Follow up with employee

Documentation:
- Manager documents discussion and resolution in file
- If resolved, no further action needed

If Not Resolved:
- Proceed to formal complaint process

7.2 FORMAL COMPLAINT PROCESS (Step 2)

When to Use:
- Informal resolution was unsuccessful
- Serious policy violations
- Harassment or discrimination concerns
- Retaliation
- Issue involves direct manager

How to File:
1. Submit written complaint to HR at hr@company.com or through HR portal
2. Include:
   - Your name and contact information
   - Description of the issue
   - Names of people involved
   - Dates and times of incidents
   - Witnesses (if any)
   - Steps already taken to resolve
   - Desired outcome

HR Investigation Process:
1. HR acknowledges receipt within 2 business days
2. HR assigns investigator (HR Business Partner or external if needed)
3. Investigation conducted within 10 business days:
   - Interview complainant
   - Interview accused party
   - Interview witnesses
   - Review relevant documents, emails, records
   - Maintain confidentiality to extent possible
   
4. HR prepares investigation report with findings:
   - Substantiated: Evidence supports complaint
   - Unsubstantiated: Insufficient evidence
   - False complaint: Evidence shows complaint was intentionally false

7.3 RESOLUTION MEETING (Step 3)

If Complaint is Substantiated:
- HR meets with complainant to share findings (no unnecessary details)
- Action plan developed:
  * Disciplinary action for accused party if appropriate
  * Policy changes if needed
  * Training or coaching
  * Separation of parties if needed
  * Monitoring plan
  
- Follow-up meeting scheduled for 30 days later to ensure resolution

If Complaint is Unsubstantiated:
- HR explains insufficient evidence was found
- Does not mean complaint was false or that employee shouldn't have raised concern
- Any ongoing issues should be reported
- No retaliation will be tolerated

If Complaint is False:
- Disciplinary action may be taken against complainant
- False complaints undermine process and harm reputations

7.4 APPEAL PROCESS (Step 4)

If Employee is Dissatisfied with Resolution:
1. Submit written appeal to HR Director within 10 business days
2. Include:
   - Original complaint summary
   - Reason for appeal
   - Additional information or evidence
   - Desired outcome

HR Director Review:
- Reviews investigation file
- May conduct additional investigation
- Makes final determination within 15 business days
- Decision is final

Retaliation Prohibition:
- Retaliation against anyone who files complaint or participates in investigation is strictly prohibited
- Retaliation is grounds for immediate termination
- Report any retaliation immediately to HR

7.5 SPECIAL SITUATIONS

Harassment or Discrimination:
- Can be reported directly to HR (skip manager if manager is involved)
- Handled with priority and urgency
- May involve external investigator
- Legal counsel may be involved

Whistleblower Complaints:
- Illegal or unethical conduct
- Anonymous hotline available: 1-800-XXX-XXXX
- Protected from retaliation by law
- Investigated by Legal and Compliance team

Safety Concerns:
- Report immediately to manager and Facilities
- Don't wait for formal process
- Employee safety is top priority
